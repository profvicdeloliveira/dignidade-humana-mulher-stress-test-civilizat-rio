# Abstract

This article undertakes a rigorous inquiry into the dignity of the human person, adopting the feminine condition as its definitive civilizational stress test. From the archaeology of the “civil death” of women—ranging from the Codes of Ur-Nammu through the Napoleonic Code and analogous legal instruments—it demonstrates that the purported universality of rights has long operated under chronic gender asymmetry, anchored in the Aristotelian doctrine of feminine derivation. In counterposition, the study promotes an ontological reversal grounded in clinical embryology and molecular genetics, evidencing the primacy of the bipotential feminine matrix and the matrilineal inheritance of humanity.

Under the rubric of Bioconstitutionalism and anchored in the Three-Dimensional Theory of Law, it posits that legal norms must conform to material reality, replacing formal equality with distributive material equity (Untermassverbot). The materialization of this affirmative protection is analyzed in contemporary Brazilian corrective praxis (2023–2026), through legislation, draft bills, legal doctrine, jurisprudence of the Superior Courts, and resolutions of the National Council of Justice.

In terms of general objectivity, the study reveals the selective and deliberate gendering of androcentric Human Dignity. In terms of specific objectivity, it establishes the current and advanced stage of implementation of a truly Universal Human Dignity by the Democratic Rule of Law. Logical‑deductive, historical, hermeneutic, legal‑dogmatic, and analytical‑propositional methods are employed.
